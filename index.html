Middle School

This was when I started to explore my interests in computers and coding. In 7th grade, I joined Cyber Patriots, a youth cyber defense program that hosts several competitions for middle and high school students. I was able to learn a lot about operating systems such as Linux and Windows, including malware risks and solutions to them during my time part of Cyber Patriots.

I also started to self study the coding language Python through Edube! Python helped me establish the fundamentals of coding and allowed me express my creativity through mini projects that I had made. After months of rigorous work and studying, I was able to finish the Edube Python course, earning the PCEP - Certified Entry-Level Python Programmer, and later, PCAP - Certificate Associate in Python Programming.

High School

I started working on my first major project with a couple of like-minded friends. We spent months brainstorming, drawing diagrams and flowcharts, designing logos and screens, debugging, and coding. After several moments of frustration and cries of success, we finally created an app called AttendanceTrack using React and Javascript. We participated in the Congressional App Challenge and submitted our app, and was able to win the Most Creative App Award!

My high school also provided AP courses, one of them being AP Computer Science A. This course would teach at college-level on Java, and at the end of the year, an intense and demanding exam to earn college credit. Of course, I had to take it. Java was a very interesting and fun language for me to learn, but I was also able to learn way more about how computers work, coding algorithims such as binary search, and useful tips on debugging and testing program code. In the end, I was able to receive a 5 on the AP exam, the max score.

I have also had the pleasure of attending the MIT Blueprint Hackathon in 2022! This was my first hackathon ever, and one of the experiences that I was able to gain and learn so much more. I found people that were so much more experienced in coding that were able to help me, attend coding workshops with MIT students, and even got some free swag! I formed a team to make a project called Flush, which is aimed to block distractions on the web when trying to focus. More info can be found here!
